The Feline Frontier: Why Stray Animals Threaten Zimbabwe's Food Security

The 24-Rabbit Warning: A Lesson from History

In 1859, a man in Australia released 24 rabbits for hunting. He thought it was a minor, harmless act. Within 70 years, those 24 rabbits became 10 billion, destroying millions of acres of farmland and causing an environmental disaster that costs the Australian economy over $200 million annually even today.

Zimbabwe is currently sleepwalking into a similar trap. What began as un-neutered domestic pets has evolved into a self-sustaining population of "super-predators." Because of our lack of municipal policing on pet registration, mandatory spaying, and leash laws, we have allowed a "Domestic-to-Feral Pipeline" to open.
